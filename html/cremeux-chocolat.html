Creating the chocolate cookie

Ingredients

  • 50g unsalted butter
  • 50g brown sugar
  • 45g cake flour
  • 50g almond powder
  • 1g baking powder
  • 1g salt

Instructions

  1. Mix the brown sugar and butter (melted)
  2. Mix cake flour, cocoa powder, almond powder, baking powder, and salt
  3. Sift the dry flour mix into the butter/brown sugar mix
  4. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at 8 hours
  5. Roll into a 3mm thick sheet and cut ~12cm rounds using a cookie cutter
  6. Bake at 160°C for 18 minutes
  7. Peel the biscuits from the baking sheet only after they've cooled




Creating the sponge cake filler

Ingredients

  • 55g egg yolks
  • 80g egg whites
  • 75g granulated sugar
  • 18g cocoa powder

Instructions

  1. Separate yolks and whites from 3-4 eggs
  2. Using the egg whites, make a meringue (adding the granulated sugar gradually)
  3. Add the yolks and mix
  4. Add cocoa powder and mix
  5. Line baking sheet with parchment paper and cook mix at 170°C for 10 min
  6. Once baked, cut the sponge cake into 9cm rounds



Creating the crémeux filling

Ingredients

  • 80g egg yolks
  • 40g granulated sugar
  • 200g heavy cream (35% milk fat content)
  • 200g milk (3.25% milk fat content)
  • 10g granulated sugar (added separetely)
  • 160g 65% cacao content chocolate

Instructions

  1. Mix egg yolks and 40g of the granulated sugar
  2. In a sauce pan, add milk, cream, 10g of sugar, and bring to a boil
  3. Mix about 1/3 - 1/2 of the hot mix into the egg yolk mix
  4. Add the egg yolk mix back to the saucepan and heat to 82°C
  5. Pour milk and egg into the finely chopped chocolate through a sieve (this is not optional)
  6. Immersion blend the chocolate mix and emulsify thoroughly
  7. Cool and place into silicone mould
  8. Place the sponge cake rounds onto the liquid crémeux
  9. Place in the freezer until the cremeux is firm
  10. After the initial firm, take the cremeux out of the mould and freeze for another 8 hours


Creating the glaze over the dessert

Ingredients

  • 500ml cold water
  • 9g plate gelatin
  • 150g granulated sugar
  • 60g cocoa powder
  • 90g water
  • 100g heavy cream (35% milk fat content)

Instructions

  1. Soak the gelatin in the 500ml of water for a few minutes until soft and pliable
  2. Remove from the water bath and pat dry with a paper towel
  3. Mix the sugar and cocoa powder
  4. Heat the cream and 90g of water to a simmer
  5. Add the cocoa and sugar mix to the water-cream mix and emulsify
  6. Add the gelatin sheets and dissolve in the simmering mixture
  7. Pour the liquid glacage through a sieve into a measuring cup (or something easy to pour from)
  8. Let it cool to around room temperature (~28°C)
  9. Pour over the frozen cremeux
  10. Place the glazed cremeux over the biscuit
  11. Refrigerate the cremeux for 3 hours to thaw the frozen cremeux
